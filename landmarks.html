The Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal, located in Agra, is one of the most famous landmarks in India and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Built by Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal, this iconic white marble mausoleum symbolizes eternal love. Its intricate architecture, beautiful gardens, and reflecting pools make it one of the Seven Wonders of the World.

Qutub Minar

The Qutub Minar, located in Delhi, stands tall at 73 meters and is the tallest brick minaret in the world. Built in the 12th century, this towering structure is a fine example of Indo-Islamic Afghan architecture. Its intricately carved walls and inscriptions in Arabic are not only historical marvels but also a symbol of the rich cultural heritage of India.

The Gateway of India

Located in Mumbai, the Gateway of India is an iconic monument that reflects India's history of British colonialism. Built in 1924, it stands at a height of 26 meters and overlooks the Arabian Sea. The gateway is a majestic example of Indo-Saracenic architecture and was originally constructed to commemorate the visit of King George V and Queen Mary to India in 1911.

Jaipur's Amber Fort

Amber Fort, located in Jaipur, Rajasthan, is a magnificent example of Rajput architecture. Built from red sandstone and marble, the fort is known for its intricate carvings, stunning mirror work, and grand courtyards. The fort offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape and is a perfect blend of history, culture, and natural beauty.

Victoria Memorial

The Victoria Memorial in Kolkata is a stunning blend of British and Mughal architecture. Completed in 1921, the memorial was built in honor of Queen Victoria. The white marble structure, surrounded by lush gardens, is an architectural masterpiece and serves as a museum, housing several artifacts from India's colonial past.
